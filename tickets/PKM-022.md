## Summary

Scrape the human tournament decklists for this competition's card pool
(https://limitlesstcg.com/tournaments/550/decklists — Regional Melbourne,
179 players, full 60-card lists per top finisher) and build
`data/meta_decks/library.json`: per archetype, the exact lists (names mapped
to competition card IDs), the **core** (cards in ≥90% of the archetype's
lists) vs **flex slots**, and tournament meta share as a prior.

Phase 1 of `docs/plans/010_meta_deck_library_plan.md`. Motivation: the human
tournament meta (Dragapult 39%, Raging Bolt, N's Zoroark, ...) barely
overlaps today's low-elo Kaggle bot meta and is the likely direction of meta
drift as competitors netdeck toward the Aug 9 deadline. Replay-extracted bot
lists are deliberately out of scope (PKM-024).

## Deliverables

- `scripts/fetch_limitless_decks.py` — fetch + cache raw HTML under
  `data/meta_decks/raw/`, parse lists, map names → competition card IDs via
  `data/cards_processed.csv` / `pokemon.catalog`
- **Validation report, no silent matching**: every scraped card name maps to
  exactly one competition ID or is flagged (unmapped / ambiguous multi-print,
  e.g. two `Cinderace` catalog entries) for manual resolution in a checked-in
  override map. A list enters the library only fully resolved.
- `data/meta_decks/library.json` with computed cores/flex per archetype
- Pytest: library loads, every list sums to 60, cores non-empty

## Notes

- Set codes in the scraped lists (`[TWM]` etc.) don't exist in the
  competition catalog — matching is by name; use evolution-line context or
  the override map to disambiguate multi-print names.
- Parameterize tournament ID; more events in this format can be ingested
  later.
- Also captures the deck-score writeup evidence: our Dragapult deck is the
  #1 human archetype in the identical pool.

## Resolution (2026-07-11)

The set-code assumption above was wrong: each decklist-card div on Limitless
carries the exact print (`data-set` + `data-number`), and `cards_processed.csv`
has matching `Expansion`/`Collection No.` columns — direct (set, number)
lookup resolved 4474 of 4475 scraped card entries with zero ambiguity. The
remainder (basic energies, a handful of off-catalog reprints) fell back to an
exact name match after folding curly apostrophes and diacritics
(Limitless's "Pokémon" vs the catalog's ASCII "Pokemon"). No override map was
needed for tournament 550 — `scripts/fetch_limitless_decks.py` still reports
loudly (nonzero exit, stderr listing) if a future tournament introduces
unmapped/ambiguous names.

Archetypes are keyed by the site's decklist title, except a small
`ARCHETYPE_FAMILY` merge table that folds acknowledged tech variants of the
same real deck together (`Dragapult Dusknoir/Dudunsparce/Blaziken` →
`Dragapult`; `Ogerpon Box/Meganium` → `Ogerpon`) so cores reflect the real
archetype rather than fragmenting across near-identical builds.

Output: `data/meta_decks/library.json` — 22 archetypes from 179 lists.
Dragapult is top at 39.1% meta share (matches the plan doc's headline
figure), core = 18 cards. Raw HTML cached under `data/meta_decks/raw/`
(gitignored, re-fetch with `--force-refetch`). Tests in
`tests/test_meta_deck_library.py` (5 passed): library loads, every list
sums to 60, meta shares match list counts, cores non-empty for archetypes
with ≥3 lists, Dragapult is the top archetype.
