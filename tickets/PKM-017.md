## Progress so far (2026-07-05)

Built `src/pokemon/cabt_enums.py` (IntEnum transcription of the engine's
real select/option enums) and `src/pokemon/heuristics.py` (priority list
of independent rule functions + `make_heuristic_agent`, wired into the CLI
as `play -a heuristic`). 14 unit tests in `tests/test_heuristics.py`
against synthetic `obs` dicts — all pass.

**Two real bugs found and fixed via live WSL playtests** (unit tests alone
did not catch either — both needed the actual engine):

1. `_option_card_id` checked the speculative `select.deck` list *before*
   checking whether an option's own `area`/`index` already resolved
   unambiguously to a hand card — a legitimate "play Latias ex" option got
   overridden by an unrelated `deck` entry at the same list position,
   picking the wrong card. Fixed: hand-area options now always resolve via
   their own fields first; `select.deck` is only consulted for deck/
   looking-area options, indexed by the option's own `index` field (not
   its position in the option list).
2. `prefer_copy_fodder_targets` returned only 1 index for selects that
   require more (e.g. Ciphermaniac's Codebreaking searches for **2**
   cards in one selection) — an under-count is an invalid action, and the
   engine silently ended the episode as a draw with **no exception or
   error message**, which made this easy to miss. 100% of games were
   draws in ~10–80 steps (vs. ~150–230 for a real game) until this was
   found. Fixed: it now only returns a selection when it can confidently
   fill the required `maxCount`, otherwise defers to random. Also added a
   general safeguard in `make_heuristic_agent`'s dispatch loop: any
   heuristic returning fewer than `minCount` indices is now treated as
   "didn't apply" rather than submitted, so this class of bug can't
   recur even from a heuristic added later.

**After both fixes:** games complete at normal length (~150-230+ steps),
no more silent draws. Verbose trace confirms `setup_active_prefers_
slowking_line`, `evolve_into_slowking`, `attach_energy_to_slowking`,
`play_setup_pieces`, `switch_to_backup_attacker`, and `prefer_copy_fodder_
targets` all fire during real games. However, a 10-game batch went 0
wins / 10 losses (worse than the random-agent psychic baseline's 20% from
5 games) — and a full verbose trace of one loss shows
`prefer_seek_inspiration` **never fires once in the whole game**, meaning
the deck's actual win condition never gets used.

**Third bug found and fixed (2026-07-05), via real-play advice from a pro
player:** the intended line is to put Metagross or Kyurem on TOP of the
deck (Ciphermaniac's Codebreaking / Academy at Night), *then* swing with
Seek Inspiration so the discard-and-copy is deliberate. But
`prefer_copy_fodder_targets` used one ranked list
(`[METAGROSS, KYUREM, ZERAORA, SLOWKING, SLOWPOKE]`) for all of
`TO_HAND`/`TO_DECK`/`TO_DECK_BOTTOM` alike. Checked each card's actual
effect text (`data/cards_trainer.csv`):

- Ultra Ball / Poke Pad → search a Pokemon into **hand** (`TO_HAND`)
- Ciphermaniac's Codebreaking / Academy at Night → put cards on **top of
  the deck** (`TO_DECK`)

So every time Ultra Ball or Poke Pad fired, the heuristic spent the search
pulling Metagross/Kyurem/Zeraora into hand — which *removes them from the
deck*, permanently denying Seek Inspiration the chance to discard-and-copy
them. The deck-stacking effects (which should have been dedicating
Metagross/Kyurem to the top of the deck) were competing with hand search
for the same scarce copies instead of being fed by it.

Fixed by splitting into two context-scoped heuristics:
`prefer_copy_fodder_targets` (TO_DECK only, ranks Metagross/Kyurem/Zeraora)
and `prefer_engine_targets_to_hand` (TO_HAND only, ranks Slowking/Slowpoke).
TO_DECK_BOTTOM is deliberately left to random — no target benefits from
being buried at the bottom.

**Result:** verbose traces now show `prefer_seek_inspiration` firing
repeatedly within a single game (e.g. 15 times in one 181-step win). A
20-game batch after the fix: 5 wins / 15 losses (25%), up from the earlier
0/10 and above the 20% random-agent baseline. Small sample — worth a
larger batch before further tuning, but the win condition is confirmed
working end-to-end.

**Still open — not yet investigated:** whether Ciphermaniac's Codebreaking's
"put those cards on top in any order" actually respects the order
`prefer_copy_fodder_targets` returns its indices in, i.e. whether the
best-ranked fodder pick reliably ends up as the literal top card (the one
Seek Inspiration discards next) versus merely "in the top 2." Also
untested: whether `retreat_when_slowking_endangered`/
`switch_to_backup_attacker` pull Slowking out of the attacking seat more
than necessary now that the engine is confirmed to fire.

**Known cosmetic issue, not yet fixed:** `catalog.format_option` always
treats an option's `index` as a hand index regardless of its `area` field,
so verbose logs mislabel bench/active-area options (e.g. a legitimate
"switch to Mega Kangaskhan ex" bench pick gets displayed as whatever
happens to be at that index in hand). Doesn't affect the actual selection
made, only log readability — worth fixing before doing more debugging by
eye by threading `me`'s bench/active into `format_option`.

## Deck-specific priorities (from PKM-018's strategy synthesis)

The Psychic deck's win condition is Slowking's Seek Inspiration
(discard-and-copy a Rule-Box-free Pokemon's attack for free). Heuristics
should reflect this rather than generic "biggest number" play:

1. **Prioritize getting Slowking active with `{P}{C}` attached** over
   other attackers once it's on board
2. **Ciphermaniac's Codebreaking / Academy at Night should stack
   Metagross, Kyurem, or Zeraora on top of deck** ahead of a Seek
   Inspiration swing — these three (plus Slowpoke) are the only legal
   copy targets (non-Rule-Box); never bother stacking the ex's
3. **Poke Pad/Ultra Ball search should target Slowpoke/Slowking/
   Metagross/Kyurem/Zeraora** — the ex's aren't legal search or copy
   targets either
4. **Mega Kangaskhan ex / Latias ex are the fallback line** — weight them
   as backup attacker/mobility, not the primary plan
5. Verify in play whether Kyurem's copied Trifrost ("discard all Energy
   from this Pokemon") triggers Boomerang Energy's reattach text when
   attached to Slowking — if so, prioritize Boomerang as Slowking's
   energy over plain Basic Psychic Energy

## Summary

Replace pure-random option selection (`pokemon.agent.make_agent`) with a
heuristic-scoring agent for the Psychic deck (`PSYCHIC_DECK`), designed so
individual heuristics can be added/removed/swapped independently and
evaluated in isolation.

## Motivation

Random legal-move selection is the current baseline (used for both the
Kaggle submission and local play). A heuristic layer is a cheap way to
raise win-rate before/alongside the BC/PPO pipeline (PKM-008–010), and is
also directly relevant to the Strategy report's "deck score" (20%) and
"model score" (70%) categories.

## Proposed shape (subject to revision)

- One scoring function per legal option, e.g. `score(option, board_state) -> float`
- Register heuristics as small independent functions/classes (e.g. "prefer
  attacks that KO", "retreat when active HP low and a better attacker is
  benched", "evolve before attacking", "attach energy to the Pokemon
  closest to attacking threshold") that each contribute a weighted score
- Agent picks `argmax` over combined score per decision point, falling
  back to random among ties
- Keep `make_agent(deck)`'s signature/shape so it's a drop-in replacement
  in `cli.py` and `main.py`
- Heuristics should be testable in isolation (unit tests feeding synthetic
  `obs` dicts), not just via full games
