## Resolution

Wrote `scripts/analyze_meta.py`. For each replay in `data/replays/raw/`, reads
frame 0's `action` field (`[deck_p0_60_cards, deck_p1_60_cards]`), fingerprints
each deck by its sorted-unique Pokemon IDs (using `data/cards_pokemon.csv` to
tell Pokemon apart from energy/trainers), and groups all 3000 deck instances
(1500 replays × 2 players) by fingerprint. Win-rate per archetype comes from
`rewards` (1/-1 per player, no draws observed). Names resolve via
`data/cards_processed.csv` with a fallback to the reverse-engineered catalog
(`pokemon.catalog.card_name`) for any ID missing from the CSV.

Ran against the full 1500-replay dataset — 0 skipped/unparseable, confirming
the ticket's assumed frame-0 format holds for every file.

### Results (see `data/meta_report.txt`)

- Cinderace/Mega Starmie is the most common archetype (18% meta share, 52% win-rate) — no landslide favorite.
- Archaludon ex (Metal) is the best-performing archetype at 68% win-rate (281 games), though a second, distinct Archaludon ex variant (mixed with Cinderace, 107 games) only hits 38% — "Metal" is not monolithic, and grouping must be exact-fingerprint, not name-based.
- **Our fire deck (Gouging Fire ex / Slugma / Magcargo ex) does not appear anywhere in these 1500 replays** — no other top agent ran it, so this dataset gives meta context but no direct win-rate signal for our own deck.
- No archetype clears both switch thresholds (>55% win-rate AND >30% meta share) at once → script recommendation: **stay**.
- Notable trainers per top archetype (Poké Pad, Ultra Ball, Buddy-Buddy Poffin, Crushing Hammer, Lillie's Determination) are logged in the report for deck-tuning reference.

## How to extract decks

Frame 0 of each replay (`vis[0]`) has `action: [[deck_p0_60_cards], [deck_p1_60_cards]]`.
Each deck is a list of 60 card IDs. To cluster:
1. Extract unique Pokemon IDs (filter out energy IDs and trainer IDs using `all_cards.json` type field)
2. Sort and deduplicate — this is the archetype fingerprint
3. Group replays by fingerprint; label each group with its most recognisable Pokemon names
4. Compute win-rate per archetype from `data["rewards"]`

## Decision thresholds

Recommend switching decks if:
- Our deck win-rate < 45% AND
- A dominant archetype has > 55% win-rate AND > 30% meta share

Otherwise stay with fire deck — deck-switching 6 weeks out has high opportunity cost.
