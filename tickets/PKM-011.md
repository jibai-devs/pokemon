## Summary

Survey what decks top agents are running (from downloaded replays) and evaluate whether the current fire deck (Gouging Fire ex + Magcargo ex) is competitive.

## Steps

1. Parse all downloaded replays, extract `action[0]` (deck submission = 60 card IDs) for each agent
2. Cluster decks by Pokemon composition (ignore energy/trainer ratios)
3. Identify the 5–10 most common archetypes and their win-rates in the replay dataset
4. Check if the fire deck's key cards (card IDs 46, 76, 30) appear in any top-performing decks
5. Decision: keep fire deck or switch to the most common winning archetype

## What we know so far (from example_replay.json)

- ShumpeiNomura ran: Relicanth + Duraludon + Archaludon ex (Metal, card IDs 57, 169, 190)
- Takaaki Matsuda ran a similar Metal deck variant
- Both decks used card 8 (Basic {M} Energy) heavily — at least 10–11 copies

## Decision criteria

Switch decks if:
- The fire deck has < 40% win-rate against the 3 most common archetypes in the replay dataset, AND
- A clearly dominant archetype exists with > 55% win-rate across matchups

Otherwise keep the fire deck — deck-switching this late (6 weeks to deadline) has high opportunity cost.
